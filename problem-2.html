2.1 Problem 2

We have seen that the dot product between two vectors satisfies \[ \mathsf{v} \cdot \mathsf{w} = || \mathsf{v} || || \mathsf{w} || \cos(\theta), \qquad \text{where $\theta$ is the angle between $\mathsf{v}$ and $\mathsf{w}$}. \] In high dimensional space \(\mathbb{R}^n\) a measure of similarity between two vectors that is commonly used is called and is given as the cosine of the angle \(\theta\) between the vectors \[ \cos(\theta) = \frac{ \mathsf{v} \cdot \mathsf{w}} {|| \mathsf{v}|| ||\mathsf{w}||} = \frac{ \mathsf{v} \cdot \mathsf{w}} {\sqrt{\mathsf{v} \cdot \mathsf{v}} \sqrt{\mathsf{w} \cdot \mathsf{w}}} \] (the equality uses the fact that \(|| \mathsf{w}|| = \sqrt{\mathsf{w} \cdot \mathsf{w}}\)). This measure has the nice property that \(-1 \le \cos(\theta) \le 1\) and \(\cos(\theta)\) is close to 1 if \(\mathsf{u}\) and \(\mathsf{v}\) are closely aligned, is close to 0 if \(\mathsf{u}\) and \(\mathsf{v}\) are are orthogonal, and it is close to \(-1\) if \(\mathsf{u}\) and \(\mathsf{v}\) are polar opposites.

In the associated {R} file you will find data on how 99 US Senators (one senate seat was not filled at the time) voted on 42 resolutions in the 109th US Congress (around 2007 when Barak Obama was a senator). Each row of the matrix is a sequence of 0s, 1s, and -1s, where 1 means a aye' vote, -1 means anay’ vote, and 0 means the senator abstained. So a vector might look like this: \[ \texttt{senator} = [1, 0, 1, -1,1,1,0,-1,1,\ldots, 1,-1]^T. \]
